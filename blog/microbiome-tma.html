    • Could microbiome test diagnose your TMA metabolism?

    NCT03582826
        Tuesday, July 30, 2019

    As we are incorporating more data from different sources for our NCT03582826 study, we are looking at the potential of microbiome for diagnosis of different sub-conditions, such as TMA1, TMA2 and other. 

         Reduction of TMAO by the gut microbiota (predominantly Enterobacteriaceae) to TMA followed by host uptake of TMA into the bloodstream from the intestine and its conversion back to TMAO by host hepatic enzymes is an example of metabolic retroconversion. TMAO influences microbial metabolism depending on isolation source and taxon of gut bacterium. Correlation of metabolomic and abundance data from mixed microbiota fermentation systems did not give a true picture of which members of the gut microbiota were responsible for converting TMAO to TMA; only by supplementing the study with pure culture work and additional metabolomics was it possible to increase our understanding of TMAO bioconversions by the human gut microbiota.(such as social support), varying conditions and situations.
