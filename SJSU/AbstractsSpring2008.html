Investigation of HSA-drug interactions using structure visualization tools

Abstract: Bioinformatics has become a vital tool in drug discovery and drug development. The effect of human serum albumin (HSA) on drug metabolism was investigated by using PDB, NCBI, Robetta server, Chimera and CN3D structure visualization tools. I chose HSA because, it is the most abundant protein in human blood plasma and it plays an important role in drug pharmacokinetics. The domain II and III of HSA contain two primary drug binding sites and it basically binds with two ligands: myristate and salicylic acid. In addition to its ligand binding activity, enzymatic activity of human serum albumin on different drugs has been studied and documented. Site directed mutagenesis studies have shown that Tyrosine residue (Tyr 411) is important for the esterase activity of human serum albumin. The crystal structure of human serum albumin-myristate-aspirin illustrates that aspirin transfer acetyl group to Lysine residue and is hydrolyzed into salicylic acid by human serum albumin. By calculating the distance between two atoms and finding the localization of other atoms we gain an understanding of the interaction which occurs between drug and their targets and we can do computational drug design. This finally leads to the development of new drugs for people with different symptoms and side effects.

Studying the characteristics of psoriatic arthritis genes and common genetic control for Crohn disease and psoriatic arthritis in the pathway.

Abstract: Psoriatic arthritis is a systemic inflammatory condition characterized by inflammation of the skin and joints. Many genes are involved in this complex inflammatory response. A list of key Psoriasis-related genes was assembled using Ingenuity pathway analysis to facilitate the identification of interaction between these genes. A disease network was build to show various genes interaction. Assisted by the Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA) overlapping networks (3 Psoriatic arthritis and 2 Crohn’s disease) were identified. Overlapping networks revealed 31 common genes indicating common genetic controls between psoriatic arthritis and Crohn’s disease. Presence of common genetic controls indicates the susceptibility to Crohn’s disease. These intricacies of disease network, functional pathways and their contribution in the disease development were also studied as an essential tool to provide therapeutic strategies for two diseases.

Pulmonary Valve Disease Study

Abstract: According to the American Heart Association, heart disease was the leading killer in the United Sttes in 2006. Approximately 1.2 million Americans had heart attacks in 2006.Pulmonary valve stenosis, a major contributor to the death rate of cardiovascular disease, is often present at birth.  Stenosis narrows the blood flow pathway to the lung from the right ventricle.  This project will utilize Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA) and other bioinformatics databases from NCBI to hypothesize potential pathways that may responsible for pulmonary valve stenosis.  I will explore all interactions among all the genes, drugs, enzymes and chemical compounds involving in the regulation process that trigger pulmonary valve stenosis.  The data suggests that pulmonary valve stenosis is involved in the Neurotrophin/TRK signaling pathway, which initiates by binding to the membrane protein, NTRK3, to activate three major genes – MEF2A, MEF2C, and MEF2D, on the downstream process in heart tissue.

Integration of the Metabolic Pathway of Gout and its Treatments Using IPA and NCBI tools
[Gout, an exploration of the Royal disease]

Abstract: This bioinformatics project integrates elements of known information about gout or hyperuricemia. Gout is a uric acid metabolism disorder that is genetically, sex, and diet linked. Overproduction and/or underexcretion of uric acid can cause the uric acid to form crystals in the body’s parts cooler extremities such as the fingers and toes. An immune response then sends neutrophils to attack the crystals, causing painful inflammation. Ingenuity Pathway Analysis and NCBI were used to develop pathological relationships between genes, enzymes, and chemical compounds. The genes directly related to gout include SLC22A12 (a urate exchanger), PTGS1, and PTGS2. PTGS 2 is seen to be a central figure in the inflammatory reaction, having interactions with PTGS 1, interleukin 1B, and nuclear factor kappa Beta. PRPS1 is linked on the X chromosome and a mutation in this gene eventually leads to a malfunction on the enzyme known as PPRP. This may lead to the overactivity of PPRP in de novo synthesis of purines. There is another enzyme called HPRT1 which works in the salvage pathway of purines from old DNA. An X-linked recessive disorder can lead to partial HPRT deficiency causing an overproduction of uric acid. Drugs like colchicine, probenecid, and NSAIDs have been used to alleviate the symptoms of gout. Inhibiting PTGS2 and even PTGS1 with NSAIDs like aspirin can cause a decrease in the painful inflammation caused by gout. Probenecid is a treatment that binds to SLC22A12 and increases the amount of uric acid that is excreted in the urine. In conclusion, while much is already known about gout, a new pathway of gout was created by using fragments of information derived from bioinformatics tools.

The human chimp twist of evolution

Abstract: Mitochondrial DNA contains the critical proteins for cell respiration. It has been often used to construct evolutionary trees. In this project, the mitochondrial DNA sequence of Homo sapiens(NCBI build 36,3) and Pan troglodytes(NCBI build 2.1) were aligned with UCSC BLAT. A 55 base pair sequence with no homology was further examined with BLAT and BLAST alignment tools and determined to be part of the 3' UTR of cytochrome c oxydase I. Rfam tool hs been used to examine the UTR and showed that this extra sequence can fold into a tRNA-like structure, promoting translation of the mRNA. More experiments are needed to examine the mechanism of regulation.

An Exploration of Polymorphism and Copy Number Variation Associated with Parkinson Disease

Abstract: Parkinson’s Disease (PD) is a chronic and progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterized by muscle rigidity, tremor, and impairment of physical movement (either bradykinesia or akinesia). The symptoms of PD are caused by the loss of dopaminergic cells and decreased dopamine levels in the brain. There are no known blood or laboratory tests that can be used to diagnose PD. However, many researchers are working to elucidate the genetic mechanisms underlying the disease so that diagnostic tests and/or drugs may become available. To date, several gene mutations have been implicated in the cause of PD:  SNCA, LRRK2, PRKN, and PINK1. This paper will review some of the bioinformatics tools that can be used to gather information about two of these genes and also to visualize the disease pathway. I have chosen to explore LRRK2 and PRKN and their role in PD. Specifically, I will report information about gene function and location from the Entrez suite. I will also use KEGG to visualize the pathway of neurodegenerative disorders. Finally, I will use Ingenuity Pathway Analysis to show interactions involved in the disease.

Pharmacogenomics and Bioinformatics

Abstract: Pharmacogenomics is an emerging field that will advance customizing drug products to each individual. The study of pharmacogenomics is critical to the individual optimization of a drug in the treatment of a disease. Single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) are important indicators of the interaction between drugs and proteins. Cytochrome P540 2C9 ( CYP2C9) is a protein that is part of the cytochrome P450 superfamily of enzymes (NCBI Entrez Gene). It catalyzes reactions involved in drug metabolism, similar to those of warfarin and fluriprofen. SNPs of CYP2C9 (SNP database) are critical in the effectiveness of warfarin therapy due its narrow therapeutic tolerance. I have analyzed two specific SNPs and allelic variances that are associated with CYP2C9 and its interaction with warfarin. CYP2C9*2 and CYP2C9*3 effect the catalytic activities of S-warfarin in comparison to the wildtype. I visualized possible interactions of these SNPs and the drug using CN3D software. Pharmacogenetics poses important bioethical questions. Patients' privacy is first and foremost the most important issue surrounding the ethical consideration of pharmacogenetics. Patients' understanding and distinction of the use of pharmacogenetics in comparison to genetic testing is one of the major obstacles and ethical issues facing pharmacogenetics. Furthermore, affordability is critical to the patients that are financially limited and will most benefit from personalized medicine

Bioinformatics tools for molecular cytogenetics

 Abstract: Throughout the last decade, molecular cytogenetics has made significant breakthroughs in disease research. It focuses on the cellular aspects of heredity, especially chromosome structures, and the identification of genomic aberrations that cause disease. Microarrays have significantly contributed to such breakthroughs, and provide the coverage and sensitivity needed to identify subtle chromosome abnormalities and copy number changes at molecular resolutions. Advanced statistical software tools allow researchers to use those data points to identify the differences in chromosomal copy number and thus provide additional insight into medical and cancer genetics. Using statistical algorithms and visualization tools, we performed copy number analysis, detected critical regions of genomic losses or gains and identified those regions correlated with disease-associated phenotypes.

Connecting genes of Parkinson Disease

Abstract: Parkinson's disease (PD) is the most common movement disorder characterized by dopaminergic dysfunction and degeneration. The cause of most PD cases is unknown, some debate still exists as to whether PD is predominantly environmental or genetic in etiology. Early onset Parkinson's disease (EOPD) has been linked to mutations in the DJ-1, PINK1 and PARKIN genes. We investigated the various relationships and similarities between the three genes. Entrez gene, Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA) and BLAST software were primarily used to test the hypothesis. There were no direct relationships between the genes, however, they were indirectly related through camptothecin, Hsp70, and somatic cytochrome c. Camptothecin's, role in the cell is apoptosis. Somatic cytochrome c has a biological process to fragment DNA during apoptosis and Hsp70's role in the cell is also apoptosis. These results suggest that even though the genes were not directly related, they had key similar features and were all associated with cell death.

 

Exploring the Molecular Phylogeny of Elephantidae

Abstract: The phylogenetic relationships in the Elephantidae family have been unclear to this day. The evolutionary relationships between the extant elephant species Asiatic elephant (Elephas maximus) and African elephant (Loxodonta africana) were examined. The main question that was set out to be answered was which extant species of elephant is most closely related to the Woolly Mammoth (Mammuthus primigenius) and to a lesser extent, the Mastodon (Mammut americanum). DNA sequences from other closely related species such as the Cape Hyrax (Procavia capensis), Dugong (Dugong dugon), Manatee (Trichechus manatus), and Steller’s Sea Cow (Hydrodamalis gigas) were also analyzed phylogenetically. Complete mitochondrial cytochrome b sequences of all species were obtained from the Entrez Gene database and partial sequences were obtained from the Entrez Protein database. GeneDoc was used to do an initial alignment and manual alignments were done to maximize alignment scores. The Mega4 program was used to apply several different phylogenetic algorithms to construct trees with bootstrap confidence values. The study was able to determine that the Loxodonta africana and Mammauthus primigenius are more closely related to each other than either is to the Elephas maximus. A complex evolutionary tree was constructed in which the remaining species were also grouped together and were analyzed for evolutionary history.

Exploring genetic resistance to HIV and the Black Death

The Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) is now a pandemic among humans. However, humans can be resistant to the virus if they have a mutated CCR5 gene. HIV uses CCR5 and CCL3L1 as a co-receptor to enter its target cells. Several chemokine receptors can function as viral co-receptors, but CCR5 is likely the most physiologically significant co-receptor during natural infection. CCR5 - delta 32, however, is a genetic variant of CCR5 hypothesized to be the favored allele by natural selection during the Black Death, and is found to protect against HIV. While CCR5 has multiple variants in its coding region, the deletion of a 32-bp segment results in a non-functional receptor, which prevents HIV R5 entry. The Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA) software is used to build a network of interactions between drugs for HIV and receptors on CD4+ T cells. New drug targets, receptors, and pathways are investigated.

This was done with the goal of idenfiying conserve epitopes suitable for use in a therapeutic vaccine. Specifically, sequences bearing HLA-A1, -A2, -A3, -A24, -B7, and -DR supertype binding motifs were identified, synthesized as peptides, and tested for binding to soluble HLA.The immunogenicity of peptides that bound with moderate to high affinity was subsequently assessed using HLA transgenic mice (CTL) and HLA. through this process, 30 CTL and 16 HTL epitopes were selected as a set that would be most udeful for vaccine design, based on epitope conservation amongst HBV sequences and HLA-based predicted population coverage in diverse ethnic groups. For this purpose, a web server, PEPVAC, was used to identify the most conserved set of epitopes.
