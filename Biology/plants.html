Short leaf pine

A medium to large tree with a straight, well pruned trunk, able to reach over 100 feet tall; typically has a small, open, pyramidal crown. Evergreen needles, 3 to 5 inches long, two or three (on the same branch), slender and flexible needles per fascicle, dark yellow-green in color, fascicle sheath is persistent.










arborvitae species

American Arborvitae can reach the height of 25-40 feet and a spread of 10-15 feet.
Emerald Green Arborvitae gets its name due to the fact that it turns a darker shade of green as it matures. This kind of arborvitae works well as a screen, as they can grow as tall as 30 feet in height and spread as wide as 10 feet. A white cedar screen does not even require pruning as the plant grows into its distinctive pyramidal shape naturally.
The Globe Arborvitae achieves its globe shape without pruning and only grows a maximum of 5 feet in height, which makes it great as wind block for smaller plants or just as a convenient accent to your landscape.
Techny Arborvitae is the perfect variety for your property if you want to grow a full shield in just a few years. Rather slender, the Techny Arborvitae grows to a full height of 20 feet and a spread of 6 feet.

Walter's Viburnum (Viburnum Obovatum) 
Part Shade to Full Sun. 15' Height 6' Width. Space 4'-5' apart for hedge. Native thicket-forming shrub with small leaves that tolerates formal pruning, but looks best in natural form. It may sucker and spread to a larger area. Fast growing (1'-2' per yr) with a burst of white blooms in the spring.
